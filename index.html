Hurricane Matthew Doesn't Stop NC Games

Two key matchups on the North Carolina ACC schedule proceeded in spite of the effects of Hurricane Matthew. Virginia Tech visited UNC in Chapel Hill and NC State hosted Notre Dame in Raleigh.

NC Games

Chapel Hill, NC: North Carolina vs Virginia Tech

VT 32 UNC 3

Virginia Tech entered this game with a national ranking (#25) for the first time since 2014 and looking to continue their 3 game winning streak under first-year coach Justin Fuente. Larry Fedora's 10th-ranked UNC team needed a win to keep them ahead of VT in the ACC Coastal division.

33,000 fans showed up in wet and windy conditions that dampened UNC's highly-rated passing game and favored the stronger defense of the VT team. VT QB Jerod Evans ran for one touchdown and passed for two more while the VT defense held UNC to a single field goal for a 32 to 3 win.

Raleigh, NC: North Carolina State vs Notre Dame

NCST 10 ND 3

Extreme weather conditions at Carter-Finley Stadium, including lightning and wind gusts in excess of 50 miles per hour led many to question why this game was not rescheduled. Despite extremely poor field conditions, Notre Dame coach Brian Kelly attempted to establish a passing game and the results were less than successful - Notre Dame QB DeShone Kizer only completed 9 of 26 passes and was sacked 5 times.

Until the fourth quarter the game had been tied at 3 - 3. The lone touchdown of the game came when NC State blocked a punt and returned it to score.
